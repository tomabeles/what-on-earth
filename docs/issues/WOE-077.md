# WOE-077: Camera Toggle in Control Panel

**ID:** WOE-077
**Phase:** UI -- AR Compositing
**Depends on:** WOE-068, WOE-069, WOE-022
**UI Spec:** SS5.3

---

## Description

Wire the Camera toggle switch in the Layer Control Panel (WOE-068) to actually control the camera preview and CesiumJS skybox. When camera is toggled OFF, the camera preview pauses and the CesiumJS star skybox activates. When toggled ON, the reverse happens.

---

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Camera OFF: `CameraController.pausePreview()` then `BridgeController.setSkybox(true)`
- [ ] Camera ON: `BridgeController.setSkybox(false)` then `CameraController.resumePreview()`
- [ ] Skybox (star field) is visible when camera is OFF; camera feed is visible when ON
- [ ] Toggle state is NOT persisted (camera always resets to ON on cold launch)
- [ ] No flicker or white flash during transition
- [ ] Manual test on both iOS and Android

---

## Sub-tasks

- [ ] In the layer control panel's camera toggle handler: check current state
- [ ] On toggle OFF:
  1. Call `cameraController.pausePreview()` (from WOE-022's camera integration)
  2. Call `bridgeController.setSkybox(true)` (from WOE-069)
  3. Optionally: set a solid black background on the camera preview area to prevent white flash
- [ ] On toggle ON:
  1. Call `bridgeController.setSkybox(false)` first (so globe becomes transparent)
  2. Call `cameraController.resumePreview()`
- [ ] Ensure the order is correct to avoid momentary transparency artifacts
- [ ] Test on Android: verify no white flash during toggle (TextureView mode)
- [ ] Test on iOS: verify smooth transition
- [ ] Integration test: toggle camera off, verify skybox shows; toggle on, verify camera feed
